Latest hero Doomfist

Doomfist is one of the heroes of Overwatch. He is a zealous and cunning criminal combatant who wants to drown the world in a future of conflict to strengthen humanity.

Ability overview

Doomfist’s cybernetics make him a highly-mobile, powerful frontline fighter. In addition to dealing ranged damage with his Hand Cannon, Doomfist can slam the ground, knock enemies into the air and off balance, or charge into the fray with his Rocket Punch. When facing a tightly packed group, Doomfist leaps out of view, then crashes down to earth with a spectacular Meteor Strike.

Learn more about Doomfist's abilities and backstory on her official hero detail page

Latest map Junkertown

Junkertown is an Escort map located in the harsh and unforgiving Australian Outback. Constructed from the remains of a destroyed omnium, it's now the home to a band of lawless scavengers known as the Junkers, led by their cutthroat Queen. When they aren’t pillaging the omnium's skeleton for anything of value, the Junkers blow off steam in the Scrapyard—a massive gladiatorial arena whose combatants fight for glory, riches...and to survive.

Lag compensation and you

Overwatch is notorious for it's lag compensation. Lag compensation means that when a player with high ping does an action before a player with lower ping (the high ping player's action gets to the server later), the high ping player's action gets checked against the low ping player at the moment of the action, regardless where the low ping player currently is. If on the high ping player's screen the action registered, then the low ping player goes "back in time" completing the action.

This creates plenty of frustrating scenarios. For example, a Tracer uses two blinks to get through a Roadhog, but on Roadhog's side he hooked her, placing Tracer back to the hook's landing zone, and replenishing her blinks. This is a common practice in today's gaming, but in a competitive game like Overwatch, this is an unacceptable way of dealing with lagging players.

There is actually another way, this method checks which action went to the server first, preferring low ping players, which would be, in my opinion, the preferred way of doing a competitive game.

But Blizzard was always a company that went more towards the wider audience, which implies that they don't want people with poor internet connection to suffer, so I do not expect any change in this problem. But I sure do hope...
